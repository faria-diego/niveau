# Product Strategy: Graded Reading Platform for Language Learners
*Prepared by: Product Designer · Product Manager · Software Engineer*

---

## 1. The Opportunity in One Sentence

A clean, modern reading platform that delivers **fresh news and cultural content adapted to A2/B1/B2 levels** — so language learners can stay informed and improve at the same time.

---

## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Direct competitors (reading-focused)

| Product | Focus | Levels | News? | Audio | Quiz | Vocab saving | Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sloeful** | German, indie/teacher-led | A1–B2 | No | Yes | Yes | No | Free |
| **FluencyDrop** | Multi-language graded stories | A1–C2 | No | Yes (app) | No | Yes (app) | Free web / app premium |
| **Readlang** | Bring-your-own text reader | Any | No | No | No | Yes | $48/year |
| **LingQ** | Massive library + reader | Any | Partial | Yes | No | Yes | ~$13/month |
| **Lingua.com** | Static leveled texts | A1–B2 | No | No | Basic | No | Free |
| **German.net** | Static leveled texts | A1–B2 | No | No | Basic | No | Free |
| **Deutsche Welle** | News + learning (institutional) | A1–C2 | Yes | Yes | Yes | No | Free |
| **Alumniportal** | Reading texts (institutional) | A2–B2 | No | No | Basic | No | Free |

### Key takeaways from the landscape

**What's already out there:** Simple, mostly free sites (German.net, Lingua.com, Alumniportal) provide static leveled texts but feel dated — no modern UX, no real interactivity. Deutsche Welle is well-funded and does news, but has an institutional feel, moves slowly, and doesn't let you read the **same article at multiple levels**. LingQ is the most "complete" product but is complex, expensive, and overwhelming for casual learners. Sloeful is the closest in spirit — indie, German-focused, quality content — but is run by a teacher and has no news.

**The gap you can fill:** No product currently offers a clean, product-first reading experience where:
- Fresh news is **auto-adapted to A2/B1/B2 levels** from the same source article
- The UX is modern and mobile-friendly (not academic or institutional)
- All key reading tools (vocab tooltips, audio, quizzes, word saving) are in one place
- The product feels like it was built by someone who actually learns languages, not by a publisher

## 3. Positioning

### Target user
Self-directed adult language learners (18–45) who are already studying German through courses, apps, or tutors and want **supplementary reading practice** with real-world content. They're not beginners using Duolingo for the first time — they're past A2 and hitting the "intermediate plateau," where generic apps stop feeling useful.

### Positioning statement
> *"The smartest way to practice reading German — real news and topics you care about, adapted to your exact level."*

### What makes you different
1. **Same article, three levels** — read a news story at A2 today, at B1 next month. No other product does this cleanly.
2. **Current content** — not recycled fairy tales or static texts. Fresh news + evergreen topics updated regularly.
3. **Built by a learner** — you're a German learner yourself. That gives you authentic empathy and credibility.
4. **Lean and opinionated** — not a feature bloat platform. Fast, clean, focused on one thing done well.

## 4. Monetization Options

Every option below includes a free tier — this is non-negotiable because free users are your best marketing channel.

---

### Option A: Feature-Gated Freemium ⭐ *Recommended starting point*

**How it works:** All content is accessible for free. Premium unlocks the interactive features.

| Tier | What you get |
|---|---|
| Free | All texts at all levels, basic reading experience |
| Premium (~€6/month or €45/year) | Audio narration, comprehension quizzes, vocabulary saving + review, offline mode |

**Pros:**
- Low barrier to entry — people can share links to texts freely, great for SEO and organic growth
- Users experience the core value before paying — they're more likely to upgrade when they see what they're missing
- Content can be indexed and discovered on Google (huge for acquisition)
- You accumulate users even with zero marketing spend

**Cons:**
- You're giving away your main asset (the content) for free; conversion depends on how compelling the premium features feel
- Revenue is slower to come; you need a meaningful user base first

**Free tier protection:** The free experience is genuinely useful — it's not crippled. Users who never pay are still brand ambassadors.

---

### Option B: Content-Limited Freemium

**How it works:** Full features available, but free users can only access a limited number of texts per month (e.g., 5/month). Premium = unlimited.

| Tier | What you get |
|---|---|
| Free | 5 texts/month, all features enabled |
| Premium (~€6/month) | Unlimited texts, all features |

**Pros:**
- Higher conversion pressure — motivated learners will hit the limit and upgrade
- You can monetize earlier with a smaller content library
- Simpler mental model to communicate

**Cons:**
- Frustrating for users who hit the wall before they're "hooked"
- Limits sharing/SEO — paywalled content doesn't get indexed
- Feels arbitrary if your content library is small at launch

**Free tier protection:** The 5 free texts reset monthly, and you can offer a "starter pack" of evergreen texts that are always free.

---

### Option C: Ads + Ad-Free Subscription

**How it works:** Free access to everything with tasteful ads (no trackers, no pop-ups). Premium removes ads and unlocks extra features.

| Tier | What you get |
|---|---|
| Free | Everything, with ads |
| Premium (~€4/month) | Ad-free + bonus features (audio, quizzes) |

**Pros:**
- Revenue from day 1, even with tiny traffic
- No paywall friction — maximum growth potential
- Good fit if you build a large audience before optimizing conversion

**Cons:**
- Ad revenue is very low until you have 50k+ monthly visits; not viable as primary revenue early on
- Ads feel cheap on a learning product and can hurt your brand positioning
- Privacy regulations (GDPR) make ads complicated in the EU/Germany market specifically

**Free tier protection:** The ad-supported experience is fully functional — ads are contextual, not behavioral.

---

### Option D: Credit / Token Model

**How it works:** Users get a monthly credit allowance. Texts cost 1 credit, quizzes cost 1 credit, etc. Premium = unlimited credits.

**Pros:**
- Feels fair and transparent
- Pay-as-you-go option for occasional users

**Cons:**
- Cognitively complex — users hate thinking about credits for something they're trying to make a habit
- Hard to explain in a simple landing page
- Not recommended for a consumer product at this stage

---

### Option E: B2B / Language School Plan (future)

**How it works:** Individual plans for self-learners + a "classroom" plan for teachers who assign texts to students.

| Tier | What you get |
|---|---|
| Individual (free + premium) | As above |
| Teacher plan (~€20-30/month) | Student dashboard, progress tracking, assignment creation, class analytics |
| School plan (custom) | Multiple teachers, admin dashboard, invoicing |

**Pros:**
- Much higher ARPU (average revenue per user) — one school contract = hundreds of individual subscribers
- Predictable, annual contracts — great for financial stability
- Language schools, Volkshochschulen, and online tutors are always looking for reading supplements

**Cons:**
- Complex to build (you need a teacher/student system, not just a reader)
- B2B sales cycle is slower — not suitable for MVP
- Only relevant once you have proven content quality

**Free tier protection:** Free teacher trial with limited students and texts.

---

### Recommended monetization path

**Launch:** Feature-gated freemium (Option A). Give away the content, gate the tools. Maximize reach and SEO.

**Month 3–6:** Add content-limited gating on top (e.g., audio + quizzes are premium AND texts are capped after a point). This hybrid approach converts better.

**Year 2:** Explore a teacher/school plan once you have evidence of classroom use (you'll see it in your analytics).

## 5. Release Phases

### Phase 0 — Validate before you build (2–3 weeks)

*Goal: Prove there's demand before writing a single line of product code.*

- Build a one-page landing with email capture (use Carrd, Framer, or a simple HTML page)
- Describe the product clearly: "Fresh German news adapted to your level. Coming soon."
- Include 2–3 example text previews (mockups are fine)
- Distribute in: r/German, r/languagelearning, Duolingo forums, HelloTalk, language learning Facebook groups, Product Hunt "upcoming"
- **Target: 150–300 email subscribers before building**

If you can't get to 100 signups with zero product and zero budget, revisit the positioning before building.

---

### Phase 1 — MVP (4–8 weeks of building)

*Goal: A working product that delivers core value. No auth. No payments.*

**Content:**
- 20–30 texts total (mix of news topics + general knowledge)
- Each text at 3 levels = 60–90 text variants
- Build a simple AI pipeline to generate the level variants (Claude/GPT)

**Features:**
- Home page with text feed (filterable by level and topic)
- Text reading page with:
  - Level selector (A2 / B1 / B2) — same topic, different levels
  - Vocabulary tooltips (tap/hover a word → see translation)
  - 3–5 comprehension questions at the end
- No auth required
- No audio yet
- Basic mobile-responsive design

**Tech stack (lean, zero/low cost):**
- Next.js (React) — SSR for SEO
- Vercel — free tier hosting
- Supabase — free tier for database (texts, vocab definitions)
- Claude/OpenAI API — text generation pipeline (run it manually, not automated yet)
- Browser's native speech synthesis — free basic TTS (if you want a quick audio win)

**Success metric:** 50 active users/week reading at least 2 texts each.

---

### Phase 2 — Growth & Retention (4–6 weeks after MVP launch)

*Goal: Give users a reason to come back.*

- **User accounts** (email + Google sign-in via Supabase Auth)
- **Save words** → personal vocabulary list, simple spaced repetition review
- **Content automation pipeline** → AI generates 3–5 new texts/week automatically (news scraping + level adaptation)
- **Freemium gating begins** → sign-in required for word saving; audio and quizzes gated for logged-in users
- **SEO optimization** → text pages are crawlable, with proper titles like "German Reading Text B1: Climate Change"
- **Email newsletter** → weekly digest of new texts sent to subscribers

**Success metric:** 30% week-2 retention, 200+ registered users.

---

### Phase 3 — Monetization (months 3–5)

*Goal: First €1 in revenue.*

- Launch **premium subscription** (€5.90/month or €44/year)
  - Audio narration (upgrade from native TTS → ElevenLabs or similar)
  - Unlimited quizzes + detailed quiz results
  - Word export (CSV/Anki format)
  - Priority access to new texts
- Stripe integration
- Simple pricing page
- Upgrade prompts at natural friction points (hitting free limits)

**Success metric:** 2–3% free-to-paid conversion, first 20 paying subscribers.

---

### Phase 4 — Expansion (month 6+)

*Add only after Phase 3 is stable.*

- **English content** — same platform, language switcher
- **B2/C1 levels** — serve more advanced learners
- **Teacher plan** — if you're seeing classroom use in your analytics
- **PWA / mobile app** — improve mobile experience
- **Podcast companion** — audio-first version of the same texts (builds a second acquisition channel)

## 7. Risks to Watch

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| AI-generated content quality is inconsistent | Review every text before publishing (you're a learner — you can feel when something is off). Build a QA checklist. |
| Deutsche Welle or a well-funded competitor copies the concept | Speed + niche focus + community are your moat. Get to market fast. |
| Low conversion from free to paid | Don't gate too aggressively. Let users fall in love with the product first. |
| News scraping runs into copyright issues | Use news as *inspiration* for topics, not copy-pasted content. AI adapts the topic, not the article. |
| Burnout building solo | Scope Phase 1 very tightly. Ship something embarrassingly simple first. |
